Tortuguero National Park

The Culture

This area is hands down the rainiest area in the country, receiving an average of 200 inches of rainfall annually. December, June and July are the rainiest months, and February, March and September are the driest. The habitat is lush and varied, including the beach, mangroves, and rainforest – needless to say, Tortuguero is home to myriad species of wildlife

Fast Facts
  • Volcano experts consider Arenal Volcano to have the world's third-most perfect volcanic cone
  • The Arenal region is also known as the adventure capital of Costa Rica
  • The town of Monteverde was founded in the 1950's by nine Quaker families from the United States
  • The Monteverde Region straddles the Continental Divide and boasts six different climate zones – ideal conditions for a huge variety of plant and animal life
  • Tamarindo sits on Costa Rica's Gold Coast, and is a hotspot for surfing and other aquatic explorations
Things You’ll Love
  • Fresh, clean air
  • The freedom to roam and explore at your leisure
  • A variety of weather, from hot beaches to cool cloud forest
  • Stargazing with little to no light pollution
  • A front-row seat to sea turtle nesting
Tips and Recommendations
  • Your U.S. driver's license is valid in Costa Rica for the duration of your travel stamp (entry visa)
  • You'll be visiting cloud and rainforest, so always take rain gear, including boots and a poncho
  • Always carry sun protection, including sunblock and sunglasses
  • Pack extra clothes – if things get wet, they won’t air-dry easily
  • Invest in an inexpensive dry bag for your electronics (camera, video camera, cell phone)
  • Be sure to tell your hotels about any food restrictions or allergies
The Environment

Tortuguero’s environment, in a word? Wet. This area is hands down the rainiest area in the country, receiving an average of 200 inches of rainfall annually. December, June and July are the rainiest months, and February, March and September are the driest. The habitat is lush and varied, including the beach, mangroves, and rainforest – needless to say, Tortuguero is home to myriad species of wildlife.

Wildlife

Wildlife watching is spectacular throughout Costa Rica, but this trip's varied altitudes and climate zones provide ideal conditions for spotting a variety of fauna. Keep your eyes peeled for resplendent quetzals, howler monkeys, coatimundis, ctenosaurs, three-toed sloths, leatherback turtles, and kinkajous.

When to take this trip

You will enjoy this trip during any month of the year, but the dry season (December-April) assures the best chance of sunny skies and pleasant afternoons. Resplendent quetzal season in Monteverde runs March-June, while Tamarindo's leatherback turtle nesting season is October to March.
