Hotel Punta Islita

Is learning how to surf on your to do list? Take the opportunity to pre-book your Oahu basic surf lesson. Learn surf techniques, safety rules and surf break etiquette under the guidance of certified lifeguards and surf instructors. With over an hour and a half of water time you will have the chance to try it all.

With its white sand beaches, Big Bay is a surfers' paradise and is home to many local and international surfing competitions. All you need today is your swimming costume, a towel, hat, sunblock and don't forget your camera.

What to Expect

Want to learn how to surf? Where could be better than Sydney, the heart of Australia's surfing culture. For a great, fun-filled introduction to surfing, take this full-day tour to a beach just 45 minutes south of Sydney, where you'll learn all the basics of board surfing from your fully qualified surf instructor.

Things you’ll love

A team of expert instructors will cover basic aspects of surfing, from ocean safety and surf etiquette to paddling, pop-ups and wave-riding techniques. But perfection isn’t necessary – follow the motto "the best surfer in the water is the one having the most fun," and you’ll have a wonderful time, even if you don’t master the sport in 90 minutes. Surf instructors are certified in CPR, first aid and lifesaving.

Amenities

  • One or two-hour surf lesson with a qualified instructor
  • Rashguard shirt (rental)
  • Surfboard equipment (rental)
  • Rubberized fins (rental)
  • Optional water shoes (rental)
  • Hotel pickup and drop-off for private and semi-private lesson participants staying in Waikiki hotels.
  • Stand up paddle lesson participants requesting hotel pickup and drop-off will be charged an additional \$10 per person on the day of lesson.
Policies
  • Confirmation will be received at time of booking.
  • Children must be able to swim one lap unassisted across a 25-yard pool to partake in the surflessons.
  • All students must have parent sign a parental consent form.
  • Surfing while pregnant is highly discouraged.
  • Bring sunscreen, sweatshirt, swimsuit, towel, water, a light snack, hair ties and any other items you might need while at the beach.
  • Kids must be between the ages of 5 and 11.

Tips & Recommendations

  • Make sure you are always on time for all pick ups.
  • While in San Jose avoid going too far away from your hotel and ask hotel staff for directions of where to go.
  • Do not go too far away from your your traveling group on your transfer stops.
  • Always wear sun screen, the sun is quite hot there.
  • Carry a little cash with you, some places give discounts if you pay cash.
  • Do not leave your items unnatended in any of the locations, specially at the beach.
  • If you need assistance at any time during your trip, ask your guide.
  • If you have any food allergies or specific food requirements make sure you inform the tour operator

By Plane

You can arrive to the Tamarindo airstrip from the two San José airports. Sansa and Nature Air are the only two commercial airlines flying here.

By Car

From San José take the number 1 road and drive 145km until Limonal. Turn left on road 18 and drive 50km until Mansión. Turn right on road 21 and drive 34km until Santa Cruz. From Santa Cruz, take road 160 to the West, drive 16km then turn right on road 155. Tamarindo is 18km away.
