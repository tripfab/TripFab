Fast Facts
  • • Birds in Carara include the orange-chinned parakeets and other parrots, hummingbirds, woodpeckers, motmots, jacamars, manakins, antbirds and several species of trogon
  • • Liberia is called “the White City” by locals because of the white material which the roads were made out of, and the previous whitewashed colonial houses
  • • Palo Verde is both a wetland and a dry tropical “rainforest”
  • • The famous highway that splices Braulio Carrillo actually protects the virgin rainforest – and gives visitors phenomenal access
  • • There are no roads to Tortuguero – you can only get there by plane or boat
  • • While it was made a nesting preserve in 1963, Tortuguero was not declared a national park until 1970
  • • San José wasn’t the country’s first capital city – neighboring Cartago was
Things You'll Love
  • • All the birds (naturally)
  • • Authentic country cuisine and the incredible fish along the coast
  • • Sampling quite a few hotels all with warm hospitality
  • • Intimate transportation on charter planes, boats and air conditioned luxury vans
  • • The beaches, jungle, volcanoes and villages – all only a short drive away from one another
  • • Great handmade goodies to take home (at nice prices)
Tips & Recommendations
  • • Pack carefully – rain gear like boots and a waterproof jacket are crucial
  • • Also bring swimwear, water shoes and any personal water gear that’s a necessity
  • • Bring sunblock – it’s expensive in Costa Rica and you’re going to need it
  • • A dry bag is a must to take on the boat
  • • Binoculars and cameras (regular and waterproof) are nearly requisite
  • • Exchange money into colones at the airport or banks, since many smaller shops do not take cards
  • • Brush up on some Spanish if possible
